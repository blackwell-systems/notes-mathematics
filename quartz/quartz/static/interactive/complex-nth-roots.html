The n distinct nth roots of a complex number

The nth roots of z = r(cos theta + i sin theta) all have modulus r1/n and are spaced 2 pi / n radians apart, so they form a regular n-gon on a circle. The k-th root (read "z-sub-k") is zk = r1/n(cos((theta + 2 pi k)/n) + i sin((theta + 2 pi k)/n)) for k = 0, 1, ..., n minus 1.
