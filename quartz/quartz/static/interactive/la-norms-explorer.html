The L1 ball's corners sit on the axes, so among vectors of equal L1 size the "cheapest" directions are axis-aligned — this is why L1 penalties favor sparse solutions. The L2 ball is a circle, perfectly rotationally symmetric (no direction preferred). The L∞ ball is a square that simply bounds each coordinate by 1.

As p grows from 1 to ∞, the unit ball morphs from the diamond through the circle toward the square, showing that a vector's "size" depends entirely on which norm you measure it with.
