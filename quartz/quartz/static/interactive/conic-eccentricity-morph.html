Drawn from the polar focus-directrix form r = l / (1 + e·cosθ), with the focus F at the pole. Every point P on the curve satisfies (distance from P to F) = e × (distance from P to the directrix). Slide e (read "eccentricity") from 0 upward: at e=0 the curve is a circle, for 0<e<1 an ellipse, at exactly e=1 a parabola, and for e>1 a hyperbola. Here θ is read "theta".
