Eigenvalues & eigenvectors: the directions a matrix does not turn

The matrix A (say "A") transforms the whole plane. Most vectors get rotated as well as stretched, but along an eigenvector the map only scales: A v = λ v, where λ (say "lambda") is the eigenvalue. Watch the unit circle become an ellipse, and drag the black test vector to hunt for the special non-turning directions (the dashed red lines).
