Central Limit Theorem Sampler

Average n draws from a lopsided source and watch the histogram of those averages settle into a bell curve.

Shape of the source (one draw)

The key idea: even when the source is skewed (Exponential) or bimodal, the distribution of the sample mean becomes approximately normal as n grows. Its center stays at the source mean μ, but its spread shrinks like 1/√n: the red curve is Normal(μ, σ/√n), and the blue histogram of collected means marches toward it.
