Modular Arithmetic Clock

A dial of the residues 0, 1, …, n−1 modulo n. Combine a and b and watch the result land on the dial. The panel runs the Euclidean algorithm for gcd(a, b), read "the greatest common divisor of a and b".

Euclidean algorithm: gcd(a, b)

a (start) b (step amount) result mod n

"a ≡ r (mod n)" is read "a is congruent to r modulo n": a and r leave the same remainder when divided by n. Addition steps b positions clockwise from a; multiplication lands on (a × b) mod n. The Euclidean algorithm repeatedly replaces (a, b) with (b, a mod b) until the remainder is 0; the last nonzero remainder is the gcd.
