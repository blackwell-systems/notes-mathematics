Gradient & Directional-Derivative Explorer

The gradient ∇f (red) is always perpendicular to the level curve and points in the direction of steepest ascent; the directional derivative Duf = ∇f·u along your chosen unit vector (blue) measures the rate of change in that direction.

Drag the black point to move (x₀, y₀). Drag the blue handle (or use the slider) to set the direction. Duf is maximal (= |∇f|) when u aligns with ∇f, and zero when u is tangent to the level curve.
