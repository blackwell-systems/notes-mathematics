Permutation Explorer: P(n, r) = n! / (n−r)!

Choose a pool of n colored tokens and arrange r of them in a row. Because these are ordered arrangements (permutations), swapping two positions makes a different arrangement. The count and the factorial cancellation update live; for small cases every actual arrangement is drawn below.

Why divide by (n−r)! — laying out all n tokens gives n! full-row orderings, but we only care about the first r positions. Every choice of the first r can be completed by (n−r)! orderings of the leftover tokens, so we divide those out: P(n,r) = n! / (n−r)!, which is just the falling product n · (n−1) · … with r factors. When r = n, nothing is left over and P(n,n) = n!.
