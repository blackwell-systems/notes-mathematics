The Whitney Umbrella: a Singular Surface

The surface z = x²·y is smooth almost everywhere, but at the origin it pinches down to a single point and the folded sheets cross along the z-axis. That origin is a singular point (the pinch, or self-intersection); everywhere else the surface is an ordinary smooth 2D sheet. Drag to rotate, scroll or use the slider to zoom.
